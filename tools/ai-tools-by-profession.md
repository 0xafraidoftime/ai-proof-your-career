## How to use this list

1. Find your profession or the closest equivalent
2. Learn the **tier 1 tools** first — highest ROI, most widely adopted
3. Add **tier 2 tools** once you're comfortable with tier 1
4. Check back every 6 months — this landscape moves fast

**Rating system:**
- ⭐⭐⭐ Essential — learn this, it's table stakes in your field within 2 years
- ⭐⭐ Strongly recommended — meaningful productivity gain, growing adoption
- ⭐ Worth knowing — niche use case or emerging tool

## How to evaluate any new AI tool

Before investing time learning a new tool, ask:

1. **Does it save time on real tasks?** Test it on an actual task you do weekly. If it doesn't save meaningful time, move on.
2. **Is the output accurate enough?** For any tool that generates work product, check its output against your expertise. Know where it makes errors.
3. **Is it being adopted by leaders in my field?** Peer adoption is a signal. If the best people in your profession are using it, learn it.
4. **Does it have a sustainable business model?** Many AI tools are VC-funded and may disappear. Favor tools from established companies or those with clear revenue.
