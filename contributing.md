# Contributing to AI-Proof Your Career

Thank you for contributing. Every profession added here is a real person helped.

---

## How to add or update a profession

1. **Copy the template below** into a new file under `professions/white-collar/` or `professions/blue-collar/`.
2. **Name the file** in lowercase with hyphens: `data-analyst.md`, `hvac-technician.md`, etc.
3. **Fill in every section honestly.** If you don't know something, say so — vague optimism is worse than an honest gap.
4. **Cite your sources** where you make specific claims about automation risk (link to research papers, reports, or credible journalism).
5. **Submit a pull request** with a one-line description of what you added or changed.

---

## Tone guidelines

- Write for the actual worker in this profession, not for a policy paper.
- Be honest about what is at risk. False reassurance is a disservice.
- Be specific. "Learn AI tools" is useless. "Learn to use Harvey AI for contract review" is useful.
- Avoid jargon the worker themselves wouldn't use.
- No doomism. No hype. Realistic optimism grounded in specific actions.

## What makes a good contribution

| Good | Not good |
|---|---|
| "Use Harvey AI for contract drafting — it cuts first-draft time by ~60%" | "Use AI tools to be more productive" |
| "Physical plumbing diagnosis still requires a human on-site" | "Plumbers will always be needed" |
| Cites a McKinsey or WEF report | Makes up statistics |
| Honest about high-risk tasks | Ignores the elephant in the room |
| Written by someone who works in the field | Written by someone who googled it for 10 minutes |

---

## Code of conduct

This repo exists to help real people. Keep it honest, specific, and kind.  
No doomism. No corporate spin. No AI-generated filler. Real information for real workers.
