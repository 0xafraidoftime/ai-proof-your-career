# AI-Proof Your Career

> **The workers who get replaced aren't replaced by AI. They're replaced by other workers who use AI.**  
> This repo is a practical, profession-by-profession playbook so you never wake up to that 6 AM "you've been replaced" email.

---

## What this is

A living, open-source knowledge base that gives every worker — white collar, blue collar, anywhere in between — a concrete answer to the question:

**"What do I do, starting today, so AI doesn't take my job in the next decade?"**

This is not a doomer manifesto. It is not corporate reassurance either. It is an honest, actionable guide written for the average person who just wants to keep their livelihood and ideally get better at what they do.

---

## A quick reality check on the numbers

You'll see headlines claiming "80% of jobs will be automated." The actual research is more nuanced:

| Source | Finding |
|---|---|
| McKinsey Global Institute | ~30% of *tasks* (not jobs) automatable with current AI |
| Oxford/Frey & Osborne | ~47% of US jobs at "high risk" — but over 10–20 years, not overnight |
| World Economic Forum | 85 million jobs displaced *and* 97 million new roles created by 2025 |
| Goldman Sachs (2023) | 300 million jobs *affected* (not eliminated) globally |

The distinction that matters: **tasks get automated, not jobs.** A job is a bundle of tasks. AI will eat some tasks in almost every job. Your move is to shift your value toward the tasks that remain human — and to become the person who runs the AI tools better than anyone else in your field.

## The core framework

```
Layer 1 — Human Advantage
  Things AI structurally cannot do: embody judgment, carry accountability,
  build genuine trust, operate in unpredictable physical environments,
  navigate ethical ambiguity with cultural context.

Layer 2 — Skill Stacking
  The multiplier model: Domain Expertise × AI Fluency × Communication.
  None of these alone is enough. Combined, they are very hard to replace.

Layer 3 — Institutional Leverage
  Licenses, credentials, relationships, reputation, collective bargaining.
  These are slow-moving moats that AI cannot dissolve overnight.
```

Read the full breakdown in [`framework/`](./framework/).

## Where we are on the AI timeline

| Stage | Status | What it means for workers |
|---|---|---|
| Narrow AI | **Now** | Automates specific tasks. You adapt by learning to use these tools. |
| AGI (Artificial General Intelligence) | Estimated 5–15 years | Can do most cognitive tasks as well as humans. Human judgment, relationships, and physical skills matter most here. |
| ASI (Artificial Super Intelligence) | Unknown — could be 15–30+ years | Genuinely unpredictable. No playbook exists yet. Focus on adaptability. |

This repo focuses on what you can do *today through the AGI era*. The ASI era requires a different kind of thinking that no one can fully plan for yet.

## How to use this repo

1. **Find your profession** in the list above.
2. **Read your profession's file** — it tells you exactly which of your tasks are at risk and which aren't.
3. **Follow the action plans** — there's a 6-month, 2-year, and 5-year plan for each profession.
4. **Check the tools list** — [`tools/ai-tools-by-profession.md`](./tools/ai-tools-by-profession.md) tells you what to actually learn.
5. **Come back every 6 months** — this repo is updated as the landscape changes.
